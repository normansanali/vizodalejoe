    Mia Hamm | Career

    Hamm started her career as a soccer player professionally at the age of 15 and eventually won the world cup in 1991 at her age 19. And she set another record for being the youngest team player in history.

    In 1996, she secured a gold medal Summer Olympics in Atlanta, Georgia, along with her three teammates, Michelle Akers, Brandi Chastain, and Kristine Lilly.

    Moreover, she got elected for Soccer USA’s “Female Athlete of the Year” five years in a row, i.e., in 1994-1998.

    Above all, she was named MVP of the Women’s Cup in 1995 and won three ESPY Awards, including the “Soccer Player of the Year” and “Female Athlete of the Year” categories.

    By retirement at the age of 32, she had a record of 158 international goals.
