“Living Single” on Fox, which ran from 1993 to 1998, was revolutionary for a number of reasons. It was one of the first network sitcoms to focus exclusively on a group of Black professional twentysomething friends without any forced gimmick to bind the whole thing together, along with fellow Fox series “Martin.” The fact that “Living Single” not only had a largely Black ensemble, but also one with more women than men in the lead cast, only served to emphasise how innovative it was.
