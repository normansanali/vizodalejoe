Lawrence Taylor Biography, Career, Movies, Net Worth, Hall of Fame

Lawrence Taylor is a former American professional player who spent his entire career playing for the New York Giants in the National Football League (NFL) from 1981 to 1993. He is also regarded as the greatest defensive player of all time. Taylor is also regarded as one of the greatest college football players ever. Lawrence Taylor Biography

Taylor was born on 4 February 1959 in Williamsburg, Virginia. His parents are Clarence and Iris Taylor. He was the first of three sons born to the family. Taylor’s father worked as a dispatcher at Newport News while his mother was a school teacher.

Lawerence was originally very good with baseball which he played as a catcher but by the age of 15, he began to play football. After graduating from Lafayette High School in 1977, Taylor moved to the University of North Carolina where he became the team captain. His jersey number was 98. He first played as a defensive linesman then later as a linebacker. Taylor was drafted by New York Giants in the 1981 NFL Draft in the first round as the 2nd pick overall.

Apart from his football career, Taylor also moved into acting. For example, he appeared in the movie “Any Given Sunday”. He also appeared in the HBO series “The Sopranos” and also the film “The Waterboy”. Let us check a few below.
